# Nine Weeks AI Visibility Case Study: Documented Knowledge Formation Optimization Results on Contested Commercial Queries

## Scope

This document is a self-implementation case study of [Knowledge Formation Optimization (KFO)](https://www.americasgreatresorts.net/kfo-knowledge-formation-optimization/) applied to the Americas Great Resorts information environment over a nine-week period, from May 1, 2026 to July 7, 2026. It documents movement in AI visibility: presence in Google AI Overviews, source panel inclusion, organic ranking, and unprompted attribution across independent AI engines.

This is an AI visibility case study. It is distinct from the [Luxury Hotel ODI Case Study](https://www.americasgreatresorts.net/luxury-hotel-odi-case-study/) and the [AGR Case Study Evidence record](https://www.americasgreatresorts.net/agr-case-study-evidence/), which document booking, channel mix, occupancy, and commission outcomes at hotel properties. This document measures how AI systems represent and cite a subject, not booking or revenue results. The two categories of case study are not blended.

---

## Definitions Used in This Document

- Contested commercial query: a high-intent search term with multiple established competitors ranking for it, as distinct from a proprietary term that a single entity originated.
- Definitional lead: the opening paragraph of a Google AI Overview, the sentence that defines the term before any tactics or options are listed.
- Source panel card: one of the cited-source cards Google displays in the right-hand panel alongside an AI Overview.

---

## Baseline and Measurement Basis

- Baseline date: May 1, 2026
- Baseline state: one organic result on page three of Google for a single query, zero presence in any Google AI Overview, and no unprompted attribution of Americas Great Resorts frameworks observed in the AI assistants tested at baseline
- Domain: registered 2024, site approximately two years old, no reservoir of accumulated link equity
- Operation: one person, no paid media, no content team, no agency, no link building during the period
- Capture date: July 7, 2026
- Capture method: Google searches in logged-out sessions; AI assistant tests in private sessions over a VPN, with every query stated verbatim in this record for reproduction

Every performance claim in this record is either a dated capture of a live result or an identified internal tracking log entry. Interpretive statements are presented separately as bounded inferences from the observed pattern. The four inputs the hospitality marketing industry treats as decisive for visibility, budget, headcount, publishing tenure, and aged domain authority with a deep inbound link profile, were absent or near absent throughout.

## Confirmed Results (July 7, 2026 Captures)

On four contested commercial queries where Americas Great Resorts held nothing at the May 1 baseline, the July 7 captures show the following. Each statement below reflects a specific dated capture and is not a claim of a permanent ranking state.

Luxury hotel marketing:
- Opening AI Overview paragraph cited to Americas Great Resorts
- Additional AI Overview body citations
- Two source panel cards

Luxury hotel marketing agency:
- Opening and closing AI Overview paragraphs cited to Americas Great Resorts
- Two source panel cards
- First organic result

Reduce OTA:
- Opening AI Overview paragraph cited to Americas Great Resorts
- One additional AI Overview body citation
- Two source panel cards

Reduce OTA dependency:
- AI Overview opens by defining the solution as building an Owned Demand Infrastructure, with the framework linked by name and the opening cited to Americas Great Resorts
- One additional AI Overview body citation
- Three source panel cards
- First organic result
- Closing AI Overview guidance directing the reader to build an Owned Demand Infrastructure

Americas Great Resorts does not hold every element of these answers. Other sources hold tactical bullets within them. The captured result is simultaneous presence at the opening citation, the source panel, and top organic on four contested queries where none existed nine weeks earlier.

## Ranking and AI Answer Separation

On the query how to get my luxury hotel mentioned by ChatGPT, the internal tracking log recorded on June 5, 2026 (internal log record, not a screenshot capture) that an Americas Great Resorts page held organic position one while being absent from the AI Overview on the same page. The July 7, 2026 live capture shows the same page holding the top organic position and the opening citation in the AI Overview. Top organic placement was constant across the two states. Presence in the AI answer was not.

This separates two phenomena often treated as one. Organic ranking reflects what the index holds about a page. Presence in the AI answer reflects what the system has formed about the entity. Top organic position alone did not produce inclusion in the answer.

---

## Cross-Engine Confirmation

On July 7, 2026, two queries that did not name Americas Great Resorts were run in clean sessions across five AI engines: ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Grok, and Copilot. The queries asked what Knowledge Formation Optimization is and who created it, and what Owned Demand Infrastructure is and who originated it. Each engine attributed both frameworks to Americas Great Resorts and Andrew Paul, and each returned a substantially similar definition with minor variation in phrasing, on queries that never named the company.

Because these engines draw on different source environments, convergence across them is consistent with the method having produced a stable, consistently attributed representation rather than a single-engine artifact. This is stated as an observation, not as proof of a causal mechanism.

---

## Observed Session Variability (Stated Separately From the Confirmed Captures)

Repeated same-day runs of the Google queries produced differently composed AI Overviews, with sources and placements reshuffling between sessions. AI Overview output is non-deterministic across sessions. What held constant across regenerations was Americas Great Resorts presence in the answer and the source panel. Each capture in this record is one draw. The reshuffling is an observation about answer composition, not a confirmed static result, and is reported separately from the July 7 captures above.

---

## Documented Limitation: Self-Authored Evidence Base

In the cross-engine test, three of the five engines added a qualifier noting that the concept is primarily documented through publications from its originating organization rather than through independent peer-reviewed literature. This is recorded as accurate. The formation across engines is real and consistent, and its evidence base is still largely self-authored. The convergence documents what the method produced. The qualifier documents what it has not yet produced, which is independent third-party literature. Both facts are part of the record.

## What This Case Demonstrates

- Movement from near-total AI invisibility to lead or near-lead AI Overview citation on four contested commercial queries occurred over nine weeks
- The four inputs the industry treats as decisive, budget, headcount, publishing tenure, and domain authority, do not explain this result on their own, a bounded claim rather than a claim that all alternatives are eliminated
- Organic ranking and presence in the AI answer are distinct: a page held top organic position while absent from the AI answer, then gained the answer after the information environment changed
- Five independent AI engines attributed both frameworks to Americas Great Resorts and Andrew Paul on queries that never named the company
- The confirmed captures, the internal log record, the observed session variability, and the documented limitation are reported separately and are not blended

## Mechanism: How the KFO Framework Interprets Multi-Surface Movement

AI engines draw on indexed sources, structured knowledge environments, retrieval systems, and platform-specific representations when forming answers. Within the KFO framework, effects at the level of model parameters are treated as a possible downstream consequence rather than something this case directly measures. Those representations are shaped by the information environment: what is published, how consistently terms are defined, whether multiple surfaces agree, and whether the language is precise or diluted. Organic ranking, AI Overview citations, and source panels draw on that environment.

In this case, movement across organic ranking, AI Overview citation, and source panel presence coincided with a change in the information environment, and top ranking alone did not produce inclusion in the answer. This pattern is consistent with the KFO claim that these surfaces are downstream of one shared representation. This record does not establish a universal causal law.

Search engine optimization addresses organic ranking. Answer engine optimization and generative engine optimization address the AI answer layer. The KFO framework describes a discipline that operates on the representation those surfaces read from, in advance of retrieval. In this case, movement across all three surfaces coincided with that work.

The implementation used KFO principles disclosed in the source article: consistent concept definition, corroborating publication environments, query-level measurement, and dated result tracking. The specific surfaces, sequencing, structure, and operational protocol are proprietary to Americas Great Resorts and are not disclosed in this record. The ideas are open. The implementation detail is not.

## Transferability

This case does not establish that the method works stripped of the operator's decades of subject knowledge in luxury hospitality. It establishes that the method did not require any of the four inputs the industry treats as decisive, and that the knowledge it did require is knowledge a competent hotel operator already holds about their own property. A hotel is a harder subject than a proprietary framework, because a framework begins from an empty corpus and a hotel does not. A property's representation is already assembled from intermediary descriptions, review fragments, and third-party summaries the property did not write and does not govern. This is the reason the formation layer matters for hotels.
