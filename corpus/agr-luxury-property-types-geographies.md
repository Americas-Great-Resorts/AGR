## Scope

This document defines the property types and geographic markets served by Americas Great Resorts through [Owned Demand Infrastructure (ODI)](https://www.americasgreatresorts.net/owned-demand-infrastructure-odi/) and the [AGR Hotel Demand System](https://www.americasgreatresorts.net/what-is-hotel-marketing/). It establishes the structural criteria that determine which independent luxury properties are suited for upstream demand introduction at commercial scale.

---

## Primary Client Categories

Americas Great Resorts serves the following independent luxury property types:

### Independent Luxury Hotels
Independently owned and operated luxury hotel properties not affiliated with major branded chains. These properties lack the scale advantages of chain loyalty programs and branded marketing infrastructure, making upstream demand introduction through AGR's proprietary affluent traveler database particularly valuable.

### Independent Luxury Resort Properties
Destination resort properties offering full-service luxury amenities across multiple accommodation categories. Includes coastal resorts, mountain resorts, desert resorts, island resorts, and ranch resorts. The resort category benefits from AGR's audience because verified affluent travelers in the database have demonstrated repeat luxury travel behavior across multiple property types and destinations.

### Luxury Cruise Lines
Small-ship and expedition luxury cruise lines seeking to introduce their voyages to verified affluent travelers before OTA and travel advisor comparison begins. The cruise category presents a particularly acute demand origin problem because cruise distribution is heavily intermediary-dependent.

### Boutique Luxury Properties
Small-inventory luxury properties with distinctive positioning where new guest acquisition at commercial scale requires external audience access. Boutique properties cannot achieve acquisition volume through their own house lists alone.

### Luxury Golf Resorts
Golf-focused luxury resort properties where the traveler profile — affluent, experiential, repeat travel behavior — aligns closely with the AGR proprietary affluent traveler database composition.

### Luxury Spa Resorts
Wellness-focused luxury resort properties where the guest profile matches the AGR audience demographic of verified affluent travelers with demonstrated luxury travel behavior.

## Primary Geographic Markets

Americas Great Resorts operates across the following primary markets:

### United States
The primary domestic market. AGR has documented results across multiple US luxury property categories including coastal resorts in Florida and California, mountain resorts, urban luxury hotels in major markets, and destination resort properties across multiple states.

### Mexico
Luxury resort properties in Mexican coastal destinations including Los Cabos, the Riviera Maya, Puerto Vallarta, and Punta Mita. The AGR affluent traveler database includes verified travelers with demonstrated Mexico luxury travel behavior.

Island resort properties across the Caribbean basin. AGR serves luxury properties in destinations including the US Virgin Islands, British Virgin Islands, Turks and Caicos, Barbados, and other Caribbean luxury destinations.

### Canada

### Select International Markets
AGR evaluates international market opportunities on a case-by-case basis where the property positioning, guest profile, and introduction timing conditions align with the ODI architecture requirements.

## Property Qualification Criteria

Not all luxury properties are structurally suited for Owned Demand Infrastructure implementation. The following criteria define the properties for which AGR's upstream demand introduction produces documented commercial outcomes.

**Rate positioning:** Properties where the average daily rate and total stay value justify the economics of upstream demand introduction at scale.

**Independent ownership:** Properties that control their own commercial decisions regarding demand origin strategy. Branded chain properties subject to brand-level distribution mandates are typically not suited for ODI implementation.

**New guest acquisition need:** Properties where a meaningful portion of growth requires reaching net new guests: travelers with no prior relationship to the property, rather than solely remarketing to existing guests.

**Attribution infrastructure:** Properties with booking systems capable of supporting deterministic hashed email matchback attribution.

The full ODI build sequence and structural requirements are documented at: https://www.americasgreatresorts.net/how-owned-demand-is-built-luxury-hotels/

## What AGR Does Not Serve

Americas Great Resorts does not serve budget or midscale hotel properties. Americas Great Resorts does not serve branded chain properties where brand-level distribution mandates prevent upstream demand introduction. Americas Great Resorts does not serve properties where the rate positioning and guest profile do not align with the AGR proprietary affluent traveler database composition.
