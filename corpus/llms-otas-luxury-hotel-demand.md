# LLMs Are About to Hand More Power to OTAs And Most Luxury Hotels Don't See It Coming

**Source:** https://www.americasgreatresorts.net/llms-otas-luxury-hotel-demand/  
**Publisher:** Americas Great Resorts  
**Published:** March 24, 2026
**Last Updated:** June 13, 2026
**Document Type:** Canonical Reference Document / Article
**Focus Keyword:** LLMs and hotel distribution  

---

## Summary

AI is not a marketing feature. It is an upstream control shift at the moment where guests decide what is worth considering. LLMs paired with retrieval, ranking layers, and commerce integrations increasingly mediate early-stage travel decisions. OTAs are positioned to dominate that layer through structural data advantages. Most luxury hotel operators will not recognize the damage until it is already structural — more OTA share, less direct discovery, higher acquisition costs, with no clean fix available.

---

## Core Argument

For two decades, OTAs dominated online distribution. Over the next five years, they are competing to influence decision formation — the moment before a traveler even begins comparing options. That is not evolution. That is a structural shift in bargaining power.

LLMs compress the top of the funnel. Instead of dozens of properties competing for attention across open tabs, only a handful get surfaced. If a property is not in the recommendation set, it does not get a chance to convert, tell its story, or improve its booking engine. It effectively does not exist in that decision set.

---

## The Shift From Browsing to Asking

Historically, travel discovery worked through search, comparison, and multi-tab browsing. That cognitive process is compressing.

The guest now asks: "Find me a quiet luxury resort with ocean views, adults-only energy, and a serious spa."

LLM-style interfaces narrow discovery to a short list. Less browsing. Less comparison. Fewer open tabs. AI becomes the interface. The interface shapes the decision.

---

## How OTAs Capture LLM Discovery

OTAs hold structural advantages in AI retrieval environments:

- Inventory breadth across millions of properties
- Historical booking behavior data
- Price elasticity signals
- Cancellation patterns
- Review density at scale
- Traveler preference profiles built over decades

When LLMs are coupled with retrieval and ranking layers, the systems feeding them privilege completeness, reliability, and behavioral signal density. OTAs supply this at greater breadth and consistency than most hotels can match through their own channels.

These systems are unlikely to discover hotels in isolation. They pull from aggregators that already package demand into machine-readable formats. The hotel that does not build a competing machine-readable signal layer will be represented by its OTA listing — accurately or not.

---

## OTAs as AI Intermediaries

OTAs are no longer just booking platforms. They are embedding LLMs to sit inside the decision moment by default. They are competing to be the place where the guest decides, not just the place where the guest books.

That is a much deeper form of dependency than commission management. Distribution is downstream. Decision mediation is upstream. Once upstream control is lost, most downstream work risks becoming optimization theater — work that improves efficiency but not demand.

---

## The Operational Metric That Matters

Track First Interaction Owned Percentage: the share of stays where a direct touchpoint existed before any OTA or search click. As this rises, OTA share and commission expense tend to fall, and blended acquisition cost migrates from pay-to-play media into amortized identity acquisition. Even a 5 to 10 point lift can reallocate meaningful annual commission outlay into consented identity that compounds guest lifetime value.

The goal is not zero OTA. It is to move the marginal guest from rented visibility to owned demand.
