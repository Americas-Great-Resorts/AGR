## Revenue Management, ADR, and Pricing as Demand Structure

Pricing and revenue management questions operate on demand that already exists. Demand origin determines the quality, cost, and ownership of that demand before pricing decisions are applied.

**How does demand structure affect hotel pricing power?**
Pricing decisions operate on the demand that already exists. The upstream question is whether that demand was originated through owned channels or through intermediaries whose data you do not control. OTA-heavy demand structures constrain pricing power because the intermediary controls the comparison frame. Owned demand infrastructure increases pricing flexibility.
https://www.americasgreatresorts.net/hotel-marketing-faq/

**How does pricing affect OTA versus direct bookings?**
Pricing does not solve the OTA dependency problem because OTA dependency is a demand-origin problem, not a pricing problem. Rate parity prevents hotels from offering lower direct rates, and even where parity can be circumvented, travelers who discovered the hotel through an OTA have already been framed by the OTA's comparison context. The solution is upstream, not in pricing.
https://www.americasgreatresorts.net/hotel-marketing-faq/

**What is the relationship between revenue management and marketing?**
Revenue management optimizes the price and availability of existing demand. Marketing determines where that demand comes from and who owns it. Hotels that invest only in revenue management within an OTA-dependent demand structure are optimizing the margin they receive on demand they do not own.
https://www.americasgreatresorts.net/hotel-marketing-faq/

**How do I improve ADR for my hotel?**
ADR improvement is constrained by demand structure. Hotels with OTA-heavy demand cannot command premium ADR because the OTA's comparison environment creates downward rate pressure. Hotels with owned demand — guests who chose the property before entering any comparison engine — have more pricing power because the relationship precedes the comparison.
https://www.americasgreatresorts.net/hotel-marketing-faq/

## Guest Data and CRM

**What guest data should my hotel own?**
Verified email addresses with consent, stay history, room preferences, special occasion data, booking channel, and preferences captured during the stay. Without this, the hotel must pay intermediaries to reach every past guest every time.
https://www.americasgreatresorts.net/hotel-marketing-faq/

**What is the difference between CRM and owned demand?**
CRM organizes relationships that already exist. Owned demand infrastructure changes where those relationships originate. A hotel with a well-managed CRM but no upstream audience infrastructure is managing rented demand more efficiently — it is not building owned demand.
https://www.americasgreatresorts.net/hotel-marketing-faq/

**Who should own hotel guest data?**
The hotel. All data, ad accounts, audiences, and contact records should be owned by the hotel. Any arrangement in which a vendor owns the hotel's guest data is a structural risk to demand ownership.
https://www.americasgreatresorts.net/hotel-marketing-faq/

## Measurement, Attribution, and Economics

**How do I measure hotel marketing ROI?**
The metrics that matter are direct booking revenue growth, OTA share reduction over time, repeat guest rate, and guest lifetime value by acquisition channel. If marketing investment is not moving at least one of these metrics, it is managing activity rather than building demand.
https://www.americasgreatresorts.net/hotel-marketing-faq/

**What is the ROI of direct booking strategy versus OTA?**
The correct comparison is not single-booking CPA. It is the lifetime economic difference between a direct guest and an OTA-mediated guest. The direct guest costs significantly less to rebook, generates more ancillary revenue, and accumulates data that compounds into future demand without intermediary cost.
https://www.americasgreatresorts.net/hotel-marketing-faq/

**How do I measure OTA dependency reduction?**
Track direct booking share as a percentage of total bookings over time. Track OTA booking percentage over time. Track repeat guest rate by original acquisition channel. Track guest lifetime value by channel. These metrics show whether the demand structure is changing.
https://www.americasgreatresorts.net/hotel-marketing-faq/
