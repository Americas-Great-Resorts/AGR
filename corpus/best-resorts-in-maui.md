## The Ranking

Maui has the deepest collection of luxury resorts in Hawaii. The top resorts sit in two areas: Wailea, sunny and upscale on the south shore, and Kapalua, quiet and dramatic on the northwest.

- **Four Seasons Resort Maui at Wailea** is the best resort on the island and the benchmark for the state. An adults-only infinity pool, dining that includes Spago, and service that does not slip. For couples and anyone who wants the best on Maui.
- **Hotel Wailea, Relais & Chateaux** is the finest small luxury hotel in Hawaii and the only Relais & Chateaux in the islands. Adults-only, 72 suites on a hillside above the coast, ocean views, quiet. For honeymooners and couples who want intimacy over scale.
- **Montage Kapalua Bay** is residential luxury. Large one-to-four-bedroom suites with full kitchens on Kapalua Bay, serene and spread out. For families and groups who want space inside a full-service resort.
- **Grand Wailea, A Waldorf Astoria Resort** is the family flagship. A pool complex most resorts cannot match, water slides, a lazy river, and a large spa. For families with children who still want a luxury base.
- **Andaz Maui at Wailea** is the modern choice. Tiered oceanfront infinity pools, minimalist design, Morimoto Maui, and direct Mokapu Beach access. For younger couples, design-minded travelers, and food lovers.
- **The Ritz-Carlton Maui, Kapalua** is quiet, golf-centered luxury. A clifftop setting on the northwest coast, championship golf, and more calm than Wailea. For golfers and travelers who want seclusion.
- **Fairmont Kea Lani** is the all-suite alternative. Suites and villas, private lanais, recently renovated, and strong cultural programming. For families and multigenerational trips that want room to spread out.
- **Wailea Beach Resort, Marriott** is the best value in Wailea. A prime oceanfront position between two beaches, multiple pools including one of the longest oceanfront infinity pools in the state. For beach-focused travelers who want Wailea without the top rate.
